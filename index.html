About

Dear Namasthe and vannakkam. Gracious welcome to this web page where I hope to share myself as I see myself with all the follies and foibles. I am Shanti Badrinarayanan. Pre-retirement I called myself an Academician with thirty plus years of teaching and leading exposure and experiences. In the fifteenth year of my career as a teacher-educator I did experience the feeling of saying, “hey teacher leave the kids alone” like Pink Floyd. Of course I don’t know his reason, mine was clearly to break the passivity of the learner and encourage them to be the agency for their learning. I started the after school programme ‘Sai Srishty Learning center’ with that as core culture. ( I hope to share some insights in the ..... route of this web page. Within a couple of years I had the offer to lead an academic initiative of a corporate. From then on I was back on the roll of the main stream education. I acknowledge the next decade plus of exposures made me an academician with knowledge of outside classrooms. I think those involvements as the principal of schools with different boards ICSE, CBSE and IB acquainted me with the wholesomeness of education. It is obvious then that I inevitably had to be a learner to understand and appreciate the system from the aerial panorama. I think that process of learning and doing kept me invigorated and eager, that it continued as a journey in my later years. Post retirement I am a writer, a poet, a budding artist, a carefree singer, a sports enthusiast, a passionate Mah-jong player, a volunteer, a socialite (not the glitterati trend-setting one) along with being an academician. I have two scientists and one cinematographer in the family. My husband was a thorough bred Olive-green armoured corp. man. I am surrounded by great friends and well-wishers with no age bar. The youngest friend turned advisor is the 15year old Advaita and the oldest one is a die-hard teacher-learner creative artist the 95 year old Ms.Rajam. I hope to share stories from them or of them, here, along with other things that keeps me robust and resilient. The four tabs on the page pans out as a window. Internally my earnest wish is to go deep and take a close up shot of my inner view. Believe me, at the same time I hope to draw you into that circle of sharing and being. Would you? I am honoured and touched by your presence. Please write to me at: shantibadri@gmail.com So long fare-well my friend In veneration

Shanti Badri
