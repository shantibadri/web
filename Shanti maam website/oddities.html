Oddities (1)

Dear You, The genesis
I will begin with the name that was given to this self at birth, Shanti. As Shanti I evolved from a peeing pooing 4lbs toddler to this petite structure of 120lbs. I owe that massive increase in volume to this earth which fed me, clothed me and allowed me to stamp all over it! It took me 62years to acknowledge that.
A voluminous 5,43,000 plus hours!
What is it that I did in these many days? From a pampered baby (I’m assuming, parents not alive to verify!) I became a child to a teenager who dragged her feet to school and went through result blues. Barely managed to scrape through all that 11years of schooling, fortunately in my time we straight entered in grade 1. Then into college with a subject combo that makes me wonder should I say I am a science or arts graduate – I had Botany and Political Science as core subjects. Clearly, I never thought about future.
True, as a young adult in the final year of under graduation I drifted to learn short hand and typing! Why? Don’t know. Hold on I have more to share. Then I did PR from Bhavans. Put two and two together I think I fancied a corporate career! But where did I land? In a school as a teacher! I never went back to my school teachers to share that news. I wanted to let them be, lest they die of shock!
In three years became their Headmistress and reigned over 200+ students and 15+ teachers, the other sundry staff. Fancy, to hold such a position when you are just 23! I remained in the corridors of the school till 58yrs of age. No, not at all because of the power I enjoyed, but because being in the school came naturally to me! I grew up once more grade by grade, subject by subject, level by level and finally showed up again as the leader in my 49 th year. 9years of leading schools I chose to relinquish my hold over these institutions which held me captive for 35+years. Phew! Never thought about that so deeply.
Aha, just when you are ready to summarise me as an educationist, here is the off-spin! I was an entrepreneur too. I had my own Learning Centre- an after-school programme and then had a gas station as well.
Still all these could be effortlessly collected and essayed, but how to account for the other roles this me played?
Do you realise, in between the tangible and accountable stuff, there are numerous other ‘unnoticed’ things that went about shaping our personalities. The non-accounted stuff maybe the extra- curricular and co-curricular roles that one would have played along with the time-assigned core role; it would also be the very many thoughts that crossed our path; the numerous big and small overlapping experiences that shaped our perspectives about ourselves and about life. It’s the sum total of all these significant and in-significant things that gives us our identity, our character and our distinct being.
But here is the catch, that ‘Me- myself’ in each of us which thinks knows all these and more about us is our greatest impediment. It is the protective proactive hindrance which at the drop of a hat jumps to our defence, pulls out stories from the closet as evidences. It is that swamp that sucks our whole being and keeps in a well-defined water-tight compartment as This is I, that’s you! And so, we remain, in that grand kingdom with our biases and prejudices!
And so did I, sinking and floating, oscillating between past and future, live all these years. Of course, there have been times when some brave passages allowed me to swim out of the ocean to the land, hoodwinking the mind-library. The gush of freedom that came during such sequence is indescribable. The novelty and freshness of such a time made me understand that beyond all these so-called experiences lies a realm that is outside of what you think, act or do. I have but just scratched the surface of this self-called ‘me’. Of what surprises (does not matter if they bring joy or sadness) I may still unearth I know not.
But if you are with me, together, we can swim deep and be in awe as layer after layer of this façade gets seen, kept or discarded as dictated by the environment. Underneath the smokescreen is the undisturbed master who through silence experiences everything. That’s who I am. And you too. As the days have opened the door to the universe, this tiny me-self understands it has the capacity to widen and experience an enormous area. It is intertwined with numerous other beings in a tapestry of happenings, that when you think of ‘self’ all other things too come as an add-on baggage. It is the thief and the police, it is the teacher and the student, it is…..
Why not read Thich Nhat Hanh’s ‘Please Call Me by My True Names’ to get the true essence.

Please Call Me by My True Names – Thich Nhat Hanh


Don’t say that I will depart tomorrow —
even today I am still arriving.


Look deeply: every second I am arriving
to be a bud on a Spring branch,
to be a tiny bird, with still-fragile wings,
learning to sing in my new nest,
to be a caterpillar in the heart of a flower,
to be a jewel hiding itself in a stone.


I still arrive, in order to laugh and to cry,
to fear and to hope.


The rhythm of my heart is the birth and death
of all that is alive.


I am the mayfly metamorphosing
on the surface of the river.
And I am the bird
that swoops down to swallow the mayfly.


I am the frog swimming happily
in the clear water of a pond.
